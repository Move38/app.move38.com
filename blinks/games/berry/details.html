Instructions

Players take turns placing Blinks, connecting them together into a cluster. After placing their Blink, a player clicks each adjacent Blink, advancing it to the next color.

Placing a Blink with < 3 neighbors

If there are 3 or more adjacent Blinks, the player must also click the Blink they placed.

Placing a Blink with > 2 neighbors

After the first 6 turns, players begin each turn not by placing a Blink from their hand, but by removing a Blink from the cluster, then placing it in a new location. Not all Blinks can be legally removed from the cluster at the start of a turn. Blinks with neighbors on opposite sides are “locked,” as are Blinks that, if removed, would fracture the cluster into more than 2 pieces.

Locked Blinks

Win Conditions

The first player to score ten or more points is the winner. However, if a player ends their turn with all 6 Blinks set to the same color, they are the winner.
